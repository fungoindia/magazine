Volume 598 Issue 7880, 14 October 2021

Protein misfolds

The protein tau is believed to stabilize the skeleton that shapes nerve cells, but in neurodegenerative diseases known as tauopathies, tau misfolds and stacks together to form filaments. In this week’s issue, Sjors Scheres, Michel Goedert and their colleagues build on their previous work identifying different folds of tau filaments present in conditions such as Alzheimer’s disease.
